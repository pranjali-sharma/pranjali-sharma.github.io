Who is she?

Hi, My name is Pranjali (pronounced PrAAnj-uh-lee). I am a 20 something year old living in NYC. I recently moved back East from SF after confirming that the West Coast was quite far from the best coast - as much as I would've liked for it to be otherwise (mostly for the rhythmic harmony). I had moved to SF after a short sub 4 year stint in NYC playing Squash and studying Computer Science and Psychology at Columbia University's engineering school (in attempts to evade the infamous core curriculum only to realize how much I would've loved it after a topical post-graduating phisophical awakening whoop). The uncommon combination of my fields of study was brought up by several tech recruiters and ever since, I have been looking for ways to satisfy my craving to work on things that reconcile my two favourite (thankful for no auto-correct in git phew) fields of study: humans and their current pet toy the computer.

I graduated couple years back and have since been looking to have conversations about using technology in the realm of the human brain/behavior. I value growth fueled by curiosity at a personal, professional, and social level. To that end, I use podcasts, books, youtube vids, and sometimes movies and tv shows to allay my curiosity. I also enjoy putting myself in situations that are not the most comfortable. I find that they help me philosophise my experiences, teach me more about myself and about the ways in which I interact with the world. Some examples of these situations include moving to a new country without ever visiting it, signing up for a half ironman without ever running longer than a 10K, joining an improv class, and teaching little girls Squash for a brief few months.

Currently, to stimulate my technical growth and to ensure my neurons maximize before my prefrontal cortex develops fully, I am working at Google as a software engineer. Working at the Big G gives me access to solid mentorship opportunities to learn industry best practices, opportunities to pick the brains of the incredible people who work here, and on a more materialistic note - great food to satisfy my whimsical vegan-first diet, never ending perks, gym, massages etc. To stimulate my growth personally, I am proactively branching out of a big part of my life - Squash. I am leaning into running, lifting, biking, boxing, and climbing to replace the void of a physical challenge that hanging up my squash boots brings. To stimulate my brain in non-techie ways, I am currently (obsessively)reading The Story of a New Name with occasional delightful detours to Khalil Gibran's poetry in The Prophet .

If any of these things spark a neuron to fire a little funnier than usual, I would love to chat with you. You can find me at pranjali.sharma@columbia.edu (I promise I don't have lingering attachment issues with my uni, my regular inbox is older than I and is overflowing).
